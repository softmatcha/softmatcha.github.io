SoftMatcha 2:
A Fast and Soft
Pattern Matcher for Trillion-Scale Corpus

TL;DR Trillion-scale corpora can be searched in <100 ms with soft matching that preserves word order and semantic similarity.

Abstract

We present SoftMatcha 2, a soft (i.e., robust to spelling and semantic variations) and fast pattern matcher for trillion-scale corpora. Our method incorporates all key capabilities of existing methods for searching corpora used to train language models: (1) preserving the word order of the query, (2) substitution based on semantic similarity, and (3) instantaneous search (within 100 milliseconds) on trillion-scale corpora. Our method also handles word insertions and deletions in queries. Our algorithm is based on (i) fast exact matching using a suffix array, and (ii) efficient pruning that addresses the combinatorial explosion of query-similar patterns. In experiments on FineWeb-Edu (1.4T tokens), our method achieves search latencies of 44 ms for soft matching and 0.2 ms for exact matching, significantly outperforming existing methods such as infini-gram, infini-gram mini, and SoftMatcha.
